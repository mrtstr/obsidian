## Definition
- It's defined for a set of [[random variable|random variables]] of the same [[probability space]].
- The joint probability distribution is the probability distribution of all [[probability space|possible outcome]] pairs of their [[sample space|sample spaces]] $\Omega_X \times \Omega_Y$


![[1920px-Multivariate_normal_sample.svg-1.png]]

## Joint [[PDF]] $f_{XY}(x,y)$
- Joint PDFs encode the marginal distributions of each [[random variable]]
	â†’ $f_X(x) = \int\limits_{-\infty}^{-\infty} F_{XY}(x,y) \: dy$ 
- $X \perp Y \Leftrightarrow f_{XY}(x, y) = f_X(x)f_Y(y)$
- $f_{XY}:\Omega_X \times \Omega_Y \mapsto [0,1]$
## Joint [[CDF]] $F_{XY}(x,y)$
- $F_{XY}(x,y)=P(X \leq x, Y \leq y)$ 
- $X \perp Y \Leftrightarrow F_{XY}(x, y) = F_X(x)F_Y(y)$
