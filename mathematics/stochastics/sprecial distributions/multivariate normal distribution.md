### multivariate normal distribution
- let $X = (X_1, ..., X_d)$ be a [[random variable]] in $\mathbb{R}^d$
- $X$ has a [[multivariate normal distribution]] if $\mathbb{E}[X] = \mu \in \mathbb{R}^d$ and a $\mathbb{VAR}[X]=\Sigma \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}$ with $\Sigma$ being [[positive definite]]

$$
\begin{split}
f(x) 
&=  \frac{1}{\sqrt{\det(2 \pi \Sigma)}} \exp\left(\frac{1}{2} \left( y - \mu)^\top \Sigma^{-1} (y - \mu) \right\rangle \right) \\
&=  \frac{1}{\sqrt{(2 \pi)^d|\det(\Sigma)|}} \exp\left(\frac{1}{2} \left( y - \mu)^\top \Sigma^{-1} (y - \mu) \right\rangle \right) \\
\end{split}
$$
