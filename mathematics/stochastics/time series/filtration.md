### filtration
- given a [[probability space]] $(\Omega, \mathcal{A}, P)$ and an indexset $I$ and the indexed [[sigma algebra|subsigma algebras]] $\forall i \in I: \mathcal{F}_{i-1} \subseteq \mathcal{F}_i$
- $\mathbb{F}$ is called a [[filtration]] and $(\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \mathfrak{F}, P)$ is called a filtered [[probability space]]

$$
\mathfrak{F} = \left(\mathcal{F}_i \right)_{i \in I}
$$
#### natural filtration
- for a [[stochastic process]] $\left(X_i \right)_{i \in [k]}$ with a [[probability space]] $(\Omega, \mathcal{A}, P)$ 
- $\sigma\left(X_k: k \leq n \right)$ defines the [[sigma algebra]] generated by the [[random variable|random variables]] $X_1, ..., X_n$ 
- then $\mathfrak{F}$ is a [[filtration]]

$$
\begin{split}
\mathcal{F}_n &= \sigma\left(X_k: k \leq n \right) \\
\mathfrak{F} &= \left(\mathcal{F}_i \right)_{i \in I} \\
\end{split}
$$

- $\Omega$ contains sequences of the length $k$ but if the [[filtration]] $\mathfrak{F}_n$ is applied only the first $n$ can differentiate between states

$$
\begin{split}
\mathfrak{F}_0 &= \left\{\emptyset, \Omega\right\} \\
\mathfrak{F}_0 &= \left\{\emptyset, \Omega, \left\{\right\}\right\} \\
\end{split}
$$
