## Definition bounded set
- a [[set]] $S$ is upper bounded by $s_u$ if $\exists s_u: s_u \geq s \forall s \in S$ 
- a [[set]] $S$ is lower bounded by $s_l$ if $\exists s_l: s_l \leq s \forall s \in S$ 
- a [[set]] $S$ is bounded if a upper and a lower bound exists

## Definition supremum
$s_{sup}$ is a supremum (or least upper bound of a [[set]] $S$) if
1) $s_{sup} \geq s \: \forall s \in S$ ($s_{sup}$ is a upper bound of $S$)
2) $s_{sup} \leq s_u \: \forall s_u \in \{s_u | s_u \geq s \forall s \in S\}$ ($s_{sup}$ is smaller than every other upper bound of $S$)

## Definition infimum
$s_{inf}$ is an infimum (or greatest lower bound of a [[set]] $S$) if
1) $s_{inf} \leq s \: \forall s \in S$ ($s_{inf}$ is a lower bound of $S$)
2) $s_{inf} \geq s_l \: \forall s_l \in \{s_l | s_l \leq s \forall s \in S\}$ ($s_{sup}$ is greater than every other lower bound of $S$)

## Definition maximum
$s_{max}$ is the maximum of a [[set]] $S$ if
1) $s_{max}$ is the supremum of $S$
2) $s_{max} \in S$ 

## Definition minimum
$s_{min}$ is the maximum of a [[set]] $S$ if
1) $s_{min}$ is the infimum of $S$
2) $s_{min} \in S$
